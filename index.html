Case Study: Emma asks Stefan to the prom

Emma was hesitating to ask her friend Stefan to prom at first. Feared getting rejected, she kept thinking about how she should phrase the request in text.

Then she pulled up ChatGPT, an AI Chatbot that basically can answer anybody's questions in a few seconds.

She wrote, “Ask my friend Stefan to prom.”

Then she typed the exact AI-generated text to him.

What happened next to Emma’s phone, was getting another text, AI-generated.

“Emma’s Prom Invitation: Accepted!”

“Dear Emma,

I’m thrilled and honored that you have asked me to be your prom date! I would be delighted to accompany you to such a special occasion. Thank you for thinking of me and I look forward to making unforgettable memories with you on this magical night.

Yours sincerely,

Stefan

an Therapist AIbot

Meet 23-year-old Anthony, a software engineer who turned to AI for mental health support after struggling to afford traditional therapy. He tried Betterhelp, an online therapy platform, but 500 dollars a month is far from affordable.

That's when he came across ChatGPT.

Initially, Anthony tried to discuss moral dilemmas and other issues with the chatbot but soon found it to be a useful tool for therapy.

"It's like talking to a therapist, but you don't have to pay the high fees," He said. “Sometimes I'll just be like, I got this issue. I don't want to talk to a friend about it because it's too personal and I don't want to talk to a therapist. Betterhelp is too expensive. So ChatGPT is a great alternative.”

Anthony even used ChatGPT to get over a recent breakup. He found the model to be a useful sounding board, providing helpful advice and asking him important questions that helped him understand his feelings better.

“Chatting with ChatGPT is like listening to a song with a hook that’s not that great,” He said. “But once you give it a chance. You can get immersed in it.”

“I feel better, that's for sure.”

While AI-based therapy is still in its early stages, experts believe that it has the potential to revolutionize the mental health industry.

With that being said, AI-based therapy is not a substitute for traditional therapy, especially for those with severe mental health conditions.
